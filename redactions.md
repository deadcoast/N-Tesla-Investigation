## RED-001 — HOOVER-SIGNED TORPEDO MEMO

The sentence cuts mid-specification. "A device not in use by any—" The most natural completion is "nation" or "military power." This means Tesla had a torpedo design that was genuinely novel — not an improvement on existing technology but a different category of device entirely. The redaction begins precisely at the point where the distinguishing characteristic would be stated.

What gets redacted is not that Tesla worked on a torpedo. That's in the visible text. What gets redacted is what made it different. Specifications are classified. Categories are not. The government classified the specification, which means the specification was actionable.

The jump from mid-sentence technical description to "information may fall into enemy hands" is too large without the redacted content. That warning is the conclusion of an argument. The argument — why this specific torpedo design would be dangerous in enemy hands — is what's missing. The argument would necessarily describe the capability.

Hoover's personal signature changes the entire register of this document. This is not a field report being routed through normal channels. This is a document Hoover personally certified, which means he personally read it, which means the content was significant enough to require his direct attention. Field memos about routine wartime surveillance do not get the Director's signature. This one did.

The most productive hypothesis: the redacted content names a capability that was subsequently used. Cross-referencing post-war torpedo and guided weapons developments for any anomalous innovation in the 1943-1950 window could identify the fingerprint of what was extracted.

## RED-002 — KOSANOVIĆ THEFT RESOLUTION

The structure of this redaction tells the story. Incident described. Process entirely hidden. Clean resolution stated. This is the shape of institutional self-protection, not honest documentation.

A genuine theft, genuinely resolved, generates a paper trail that has nothing sensitive in it: agent found materials, materials returned, no further action. The fact that the resolution is as heavily redacted as the incident means the resolution itself was not clean. Either not everything was recovered, or the process of recovery revealed something about US government handling that couldn't be disclosed.

There is a third possibility that deserves attention. Kosanović may not have been stealing — he may have been documenting. As the legal heir, he had standing to examine the estate. If he discovered, during that examination, that materials had already been extracted by US agents before he arrived, his "theft" of certain items may have been an attempt to preserve evidence of prior extraction. In that reading, the FBI's theft allegation inverts the actual sequence: they extracted first, then accused the heir of theft when he tried to document what was missing.

This is not the only reading. But the heavily redacted resolution is consistent with it. A clean "he stole, we recovered" doesn't need redaction. "He discovered we had already taken things and we needed to manage that discovery" does.

## RED-003 — TRUMP EVALUATION

The procedural impossibility is the content. There is no gap to analyze here in the traditional sense — no blacked-out page. The gap is the evaluation itself: a physically incoherent timeframe producing an authoritative conclusion that ended all further official inquiry.

The missing document is the authorization memo. Before Trump arrived, someone told him: what he was looking for, what level of detail was expected, what the desired output was, and — critically — what subset of materials he would actually examine. That briefing document does not exist in the released files.

Consider the timeline: Tesla dies January 7 or 8. Trump begins evaluation January 8. That is essentially same-day. For Trump to begin evaluating on January 8, he had to be briefed, transported, and set up with the materials on January 8. The authorization had to precede Tesla's death or occur within hours of it. This means someone had a plan for the Tesla papers before Tesla died, or someone made extremely rapid decisions within hours of the death with pre-established protocols.

Either the evaluation plan was prepared in advance — anticipating Tesla's imminent death — or the response was so practiced that it could be executed within hours. Neither is consistent with "routine wartime property management."

The hypothesis that Trump evaluated a pre-filtered subset rather than all 80 trunks is the most parsimonious explanation. It resolves the physical impossibility, explains the Craigee contradiction (Craigee may have accessed different — more complete — material), and accounts for the authorization document's absence from released files.

## RED-004 — 20 MISSING TRUNKS

This is the most concrete anomaly in the entire file. It is not a redacted sentence or a suspicious evaluation. It is a physical inventory discrepancy in a federal agency whose legal mandate was precise inventory management.

The 8-year custody gap is the operational window. Between January 1943 and September 1951, the OAPC held Tesla's materials. During that 8 years, the Cold War began, the Soviet atomic program was revealed, classified weapons research expanded enormously, and directed energy weapons went from theoretical to active research. Every one of those developments increased the value of specific Tesla material.

The inventory discrepancy almost certainly maps onto the most recent and most developed portion of Tesla's work. His pre-1920 material was largely published — patents, papers, demonstrations. Its value was already in the public domain. His post-1928 private notebooks, particularly his 1934-1943 work on teleforce, torpedo guidance, and wireless energy transmission — that material had never been published, had no public record, and was irreplaceable.

The 20 missing trunks are almost certainly weighted toward that final period. This is testable: the Belgrade museum's 160,000 documents are catalogued. If they skew heavily toward Tesla's earlier career and contain relatively little from 1928-1943, that distribution is the fingerprint of what was extracted.
