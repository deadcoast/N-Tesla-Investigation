## PAT-001 — THE 3-DAY DISPOSAL

When a government entity needs to officially close a file on something it intends to retain or absorb, it commissions a brief documented evaluation that produces a negative finding. The evaluation must be official enough to cite in future denials, fast enough to precede outside scrutiny, and performed by someone with credentials but motivated conclusions.

The document this produces becomes the permanent public record. The actual disposition of the material happens separately, under different classification, by different personnel. The negative evaluation provides the plausible deniability layer — permanently.

This pattern has historical analogues. Operation Paperclip involved cursory "denazification" evaluations of German scientists before absorption into US programs. The 1947 Roswell weather balloon explanation was produced same-day. The CIA's official MKULTRA termination in 1973 preceded documented continuation under different program names. In every case, the evaluation is not an assessment of the material — it is the administrative mechanism for reclassifying the material under new authority while officially closing the old file.

Tesla's case fits this template exactly. The evaluation was two days. The evaluator had a conflict of interest. The conclusion was absolute. No second opinion was commissioned despite documented internal disagreement. The official file was closed. Whatever happened to the 20 missing trunks happened under a different file number.

## PAT-002 — THE MOTIVATED EVALUATOR

Institutions needing specific outcomes from evaluations select evaluators whose existing biases align with the desired conclusion. This is more reliable than bribery because the evaluator genuinely believes their conclusion. There is no corrupt transaction. The evaluator's credentials remain intact. The outcome is self-consistent.

The selector does not say "find nothing." They choose the person most likely to find nothing, based on existing animus, competitive instinct, or worldview. This is not conspiracy. It is bureaucratic intelligence — the kind of institutional IQ that produces reliable outcomes without leaving traces of coordination.

Trump's Van de Graaff humiliation by Tesla is not a minor anecdote. In the academic and engineering culture of 1943 MIT, being publicly demonstrated as inefficient by your work's direct competitor was a career-defining wound. A man with that wound, given authority to assess his humiliator's life work, will find nothing of value. Not because he is corrupt but because that is how wounded professional pride operates under the guise of rigorous evaluation.

The Einstein parallel is instructive. When Einstein's colleagues dismissed his unified field theory in his final decades, they were not lying. They genuinely believed it was misguided. Their prior commitment to quantum mechanics made them incapable of neutral assessment. Institutional bias produces sincere conclusions. Sincere conclusions are harder to expose than corrupt ones.

## PAT-003 — THE CONVENIENT THEFT NARRATIVE

Accusing the heir of theft accomplishes multiple functions simultaneously without requiring the accusation to be proven or prosecuted. It discredits the heir as a reliable witness to what existed. It provides legal justification for extended custody. It creates a documented incident that can be cited to explain any subsequent discrepancy. And it positions the institution as victim rather than actor.

The Kosanović case fits the template and then departs from it in an interesting way. The theft allegation was filed. The resolution was heavily redacted. Kosanović was never prosecuted. He remained a diplomat and successfully repatriated the estate. This departure suggests the allegation served its immediate purpose — justifying continued OAPC custody during the critical 1943 period — without needing to be sustained as a prosecution.

The behavioral tell is in the redaction of the resolution rather than the incident. If the theft was real and cleanly resolved, the resolution has nothing sensitive to hide. Redacting the resolution means the resolution revealed something. The most likely revelation: what Kosanović accessed showed him evidence of prior US extraction, and managing that revelation required classified handling of the resolution itself.

## PAT-004 — THE INVENTORY DISAPPEARANCE

In classified material extraction, the cleanest method is not destruction but administrative reclassification. Material is not taken — it is retained under different authority. The original inventory reflects the full count. The transfer inventory reflects the post-extraction count. The gap is explained as administrative error, packing consolidation, or left without explanation.

There is no theft. There is no crime. There is a discrepancy in paperwork, and paperwork discrepancies in federal agencies during wartime are unremarkable. The genius of this method is its invisibility — not invisible to careful scrutiny, but invisible to casual review, which is all most records management gets.

Twenty trunks is not a rounding error. It is twenty physical objects, catalogued at intake, absent at transfer, with eight years of federal custody in between. The OAPC had legal and administrative obligation to account for every item of enemy alien property. The absence of a reconciliation document in the released files is not a filing oversight. It is a classified document.

That classified document names either a transfer recipient, a destruction authorizer, or a reclassification authority. Any of those three possibilities names the destination of the 20 trunks. LED-005 exists to find that document.

## PAT-005 — THE REPUTATIONAL PRE-EMPTIVE STRIKE

The most durable form of suppression is not silencing — it is pre-emptive dismissal. Once a person is labeled eccentric, their legitimate claims are dismissed as more eccentricity without examination. The label does the suppression automatically, perpetually, without further intervention.

Tesla's press trajectory is the textbook case. Before 1904: celebrated genius, front-page inventor, Westinghouse's champion. After 1904: gradual withdrawal of serious coverage, increasing emphasis on personal quirks, eventual framing of all later claims as the output of a declining mind. By 1934, when he made his most specific and arguably most credible weapons claims, the press had already spent thirty years priming the audience to discount him.

The origin point of the framing shift is researchable. Newspaper archives are indexed. Finding the first publication that used "eccentric" as the primary Tesla descriptor — and who published it — identifies the institution that initiated the framing. That institution will have a traceable connection to either Morgan financial interests or Edison infrastructure interests, or both.

The pattern also explains why the FBI evaluation needed to be dismissive rather than simply classified. A classified evaluation would acknowledge value. A dismissive evaluation destroys value. The reputational strike, begun in 1904, was completed posthumously by Trump's memo in 1943. The man was not just stopped — he was made not to have mattered.

## PAT-006 — THE PROTECTIVE RESPONSE

People who love or deeply admire someone who has been wronged do things they would not normally do. They persist past reasonable limits. They preserve things carefully. They speak more than is comfortable. This is not heroism — it is the irrational logic of grief and loyalty, the same primal system that makes a man protective when his partner is threatened.

Tesla's small circle of genuine supporters — Swezey, Johnson, Kosanović — all exhibited this pattern. Swezey wrote to Hoover for years demanding papers back. He donated his personal Tesla collection to the Smithsonian. Kosanović built a museum against political odds in a country that had just survived Nazi occupation and was rebuilding under a communist government. Johnson's family correspondence was specifically sought by researchers.

People protecting something leave traces. The more irrational and persistent the protection, the more they were protecting. Swezey's Smithsonian collection almost certainly contains more than has been publicly analyzed. He was Tesla's closest friend in the final years. He knew what Tesla was working on. His FBI file, if it exists — LED-004 — may reveal what the government thought he knew, which is a proxy for what he actually knew.

The protective response pattern is also the reason the Einstein/Lieserl parallel works as a methodological analogy. Lieserl was discovered not through official records but through private love letters — the documents that protective people keep and the documents that emotional events generate. Tesla's suppression generated the same kind of material. It exists somewhere in the collections of people who cared too much to stay quiet.
