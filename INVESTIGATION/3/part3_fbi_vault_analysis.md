## CRITICAL FINDING #1: THE AMTORG CONTRACT — SIGNED AND PAID

**Page 44 — Trump Exhibit Q (abstract in Trump's evaluation):**

This is the most operationally significant document in all three parts. Trump's abstract describes:

> "An agreement dated April 20, 1935, between Nikola Tesla and the Amtorg Trading Corporation, in which Tesla agreed to supply plans, specifications, and complete information on a method and apparatus for producing high voltages up to fifty million volts, for producing very small particles in a tube open to air, for increasing the charge of the particles to the full voltage of the high potential terminal, and for projecting the particles to distances of a hundred miles or more. The maximum speed of the particles was specified as not less than 350 miles per second. **The receipt of $25,000 fee for this disclosure was acknowledged in this agreement**, which was signed by Nikola Tesla and by A. Bertenlen of the Amtorg Trading Corporation."

**Assessment — this is the operational center of the entire suppression narrative:**

Tesla did not merely correspond with Soviet engineers. He **signed a contract** with Amtorg (the Soviet intelligence-adjacent trade corporation) and **received $25,000** for delivering teleforce specifications. The contract specified particle projection to distances of 100+ miles at 350 miles per second minimum — this is explicitly the death ray / teleforce device.

Trump then attempts to dismiss the significance: he says the method described in the contract is "apparently that described in Exhibit F" (the open-ended vacuum tube design) and that "Exhibit D, above, is [an] effort by Tesla to clear up the questions raised by Soviet engineers after the subject disclosure had been made" — i.e., the November 1935 Amtorg reply memo was Tesla **following up on a paid contract** not a casual scientific exchange.

Trump's conclusion is that the disclosure "subsequently proved unworkable." But Trump's own evaluation is limited to materials already in Manhattan Warehouse custody. He explicitly excluded the hotel basement trunks (10 years untouched). The working model in the Governor Clinton Hotel safe deposit box was not examined. The 30 barrels/bundles pre-1934 were outside his scope.

**The Amtorg contract establishes:** Tesla received $25,000 from Soviet intelligence infrastructure in 1935 for teleforce specifications. By 1943, the same papers containing this contract were seized by APC and microfilmed by Naval Intelligence. The 1983 classified FBI memo (Part 1) noted the Soviets had access to Tesla papers that influenced their "early research into directed energy weapons." The Amtorg contract is the documented transaction that begins that chain.

Trump's dismissal ("subsequently proved unworkable") does not address whether the Soviets found it workable. Their directed energy weapons programs in the 1970s-80s suggest they did not simply shelve Tesla's ideas.

## CRITICAL FINDING #2: HARVEY RATH RE-INTERVIEW — THE CONTROLLED DENIAL

**Pages 58-60 — February 8, 1951 interview of Harvey Rath:**

This is the FBI's document of Rath formally denying Bergstresser's allegations. His denial is carefully bounded in ways that matter forensically.

**What Rath denied:**
- That he told Bergstresser the FBI was "stopped from a higher level"
- That he told Bergstresser "their hands were tied"
- That he told Bergstresser "the last hope was Congress"
- That he asked Bergstresser not to contact him "because he had a wife and family"

**What Rath did NOT deny:**
- That Bergstresser contacted him after his resignation
- That he told Bergstresser to contact the New York office since he was no longer with the FBI
- That he may have mentioned his wife, family, and New Jersey home as reasons for leaving
- **That he made any similar statements to Spanel**

The FBI's internal document from 1951 (Part 2, Nichols memo) separately quotes Spanel's communications to Senators in which Spanel says Harvey Rath told him those things — not Bergstresser. The FBI's investigation of Rath focused entirely on what he said to Bergstresser. It did not ask Rath about his conversations with Spanel.

**This is the controlled denial structure:** Interview the former agent about what he said to the less credible party (Bergstresser, a government informant of non-specific reliability), establish his denial on the record, close the loop. Don't ask what he told Spanel, who is a named corporate executive who communicated directly to Senators. The Rath re-interview is thorough where it needs to be thorough and silent where silence serves the Bureau.

The one anomaly: the Nichols memo (Part 2) says Rath "entered on duty February 16, 1942, resigned April 5, 1946." Rath's own account in the re-interview says he "left the Bureau in May, 1945." This is a discrepancy of approximately one year. If Rath left in May 1945, his departure was immediately after the war in Europe ended and before the October 1945 Bergstresser report (which was written by Rath himself per the report header). The file number discrepancy suggests either the Nichols memo or Rath's own recollection is inaccurate — or there was a different departure date than officially recorded.

## CRITICAL FINDING #3: MANHATTAN WAREHOUSE FLOOR SUPERVISOR — EYEWITNESS ACCOUNT

**Page 52 — April 7, 1950 interview with Michael King, Manhattan Storage:**

Michael King: floor supervisor for approximately 10 years on the floor where Tesla effects were stored.

Key testimony:
- **Only one examination** ever occurred: January 26-27, 1943
- "Numerous photographs were taken by the examiners"
- **"His description of the equipment used would tend to show that a microfilm reproduction was made of some of the papers"**
- **"Several of the group taking the examination wore U.S. Navy uniforms"**
- The civilian members were identified to him only as **"FEDERAL AUTHORITIES"**
- No other microfilming occurred after that date

King is the on-the-ground witness. He saw Navy uniforms. The civilians were introduced as "Federal Authorities" without further identification. The FBI agents interviewing him in 1950 specifically told him the FBI had not instigated the examination and "the Bureau had not taken part in that examination."

This document triangulates perfectly: ONI (Willis George + Navy enlisted personnel) + APC civilians + MIT technical aide (Trump), all presenting to the warehouse supervisor as a unified federal operation. The FBI's 1950 denial to King was technically accurate (the Bureau didn't do it) while obscuring the operational picture.

**What King told them Kosanovich had heard:** The March 29, 1950 Kosanovich memo (page 51) shows Kosanovich specifically told the FBI that Manhattan Storage told him the FBI had made microfilms of all the papers. King's 1950 interview shows who was actually present (Navy uniforms). Kosanovich's source was wrong about which agency — the warehouse staff remembered "federal authorities" and apparently assumed FBI. The Navy's operational security worked: even the facility's own floor supervisor didn't know which agency he was dealing with.

## CRITICAL FINDING #4: FITZGERALD TIMELINE — SPANEL CONTROLLED HIM

**Pages 45-47 — Bergstresser October 1945 report:**

The Bergstresser report adds operational texture to the Spanel-Fitzgerald relationship that changes the interpretation:

- Fitzgerald met Spanel in **December 1942** at an American Society of Mechanical Engineers meeting — one month before Tesla's death
- Spanel provided financial aid and they maintained "closest contact" for considerable time
- Fitzgerald had lined up a deal to sell his anti-tank gun design to **Remington Arms** — Spanel **blocked this deal** by contacting Remington's top management
- Spanel then obtained a job for Fitzgerald at Higgins Ship Building in New Orleans with a contract where **60% of profits would go to Spanel**
- In November 1943, Fitzgerald was fired from Higgins — attributed to Spanel
- September 1944: Fitzgerald was drafted into the Army

**Assessment:** Spanel had a controlling financial relationship with Fitzgerald before Tesla's death. He blocked Fitzgerald's independent arms work. He channeled Fitzgerald into a contract that gave Spanel majority of the profits. When that arrangement apparently broke down, Fitzgerald was fired and soon drafted. By October 1945, Fitzgerald was a Private at Wright Field despite directing a classified research project — he couldn't get commissioned because of the investigation into Spanel associations.

Spanel had motive (financial), means (political connections), and documented behavior (blocking Fitzgerald's Remington deal, controlling his employment) to manage what information about Tesla's work was preserved, shared, and accessible to government channels.

**Q-024 [NEW]:** Spanel's role as controlling financial party for Fitzgerald's work should be traced through the Remington Arms correspondence and Higgins Ship Building contract records ca. 1942-1943.

## CRITICAL FINDING #5: THE 75 TRUNKS CONFIRMATION WITH CHARLOTTE MUZAR

**Page 48 — October 1945 Bergstresser report:**

> "The effects of NIKOLA TESLA are contained in some 75 packing cases and trunks and are presently under seal by the New York State Department of Taxation. It was learned that the rental for this storage, which approximates $15 per month, is being paid by one CARLOTTE MUZAR, 134 East 63rd Street, New York, New York; who is listed as the agent for SAVA KOSANOVICH."

This directly resolves Q-018. The 75 count (October 1945) is the active APC-sealed inventory. The 30 barrels/bundles mentioned in the January 12, 1943 teletype as being "in the warehouse since about 1934" appear to be separately counted — making the actual total closer to 105 items. Or the 75 count is an imprecise field estimate that includes both the Hotel New Yorker transfer and some of the warehouse barrels. The discrepancy between 80 (Tesla's statement to Fitzgerald), 75 (1945 count), 60 (Belgrade shipment 1951) remains unresolved.

Charlotte Muzar (LED-012) is confirmed as Kosanovich's estate agent paying storage — this is the administrative trail for who maintained access authorization.

## CRITICAL FINDING #6: TRUMP SCOPE LIMITATION — PRECISE TEXT

**Pages 37-38 — Trump letter, January 30, 1943:**

The complete text confirms what was summarized in Part 1:

> "For reasons indicated below, no investigation was made of material in trunks which had remained untouched in the basement of the New Yorker Hotel for ten years prior to Dr. Tesla's death."

And:

> "It should be no discredit to this distinguished engineer and scientist whose solid contributions to the electrical art were made at the beginning of the present century to report that **his thoughts and efforts during at least the past fifteen years were primarily of a speculative, philosophical, and somewhat promotional character** — often concerned with the production and wireless transmission of power — but did not include new, groundbreaking principles or methods for realizing such results."

"At least the past fifteen years" = 1928-1943. This precisely covers the period of Tesla's teleforce development work, his Amtorg contract (1935), his British government offers (1936-1938), and his New York Times interview about teleforce (1940).

Trump's dismissal of this entire period as "speculative, philosophical, and somewhat promotional" while simultaneously abstracting documents that include a signed $25,000 contract with Soviet intelligence infrastructure is the central contradiction of the evaluation. The contractual reality documented in Exhibit Q directly contradicts the characterization.

## CRITICAL FINDING #7: 1957 FILE — ACTIVE SURVEILLANCE 14 YEARS LATER

**Pages 1-4, 61-64 — 1957 Margaret Storm / Interplanetary Sessions Newsletter:**

In July 1957, a Yugoslav-American informant identified only as SAVO provided the New York office with Margaret Storm's newsletter claiming Tesla was a Venusian who had built a radio set for interplanetary communication. The FBI filed this under the Tesla case file (NY 65-12290).

The significance is not the content of Storm's claims — which are clearly fringe material. The significance is that the Tesla case file was **still active in 1957**, 14 years after Tesla's death. The FBI was still monitoring who was discussing Tesla, what claims were being made about his work, and who was circulating Tesla-related material.

Storm's newsletter was sent to the FBI's Los Angeles office for information purposes. The investigating agent provided a detailed biographical summary of Tesla in the report — the same facts the FBI had been providing in response to public inquiries for 14 years. The case wasn't closed; it was being actively managed.

**The operational picture this confirms:** The FBI's role in the Tesla case was never about investigating anything. From 1943 onwards it was about monitoring the ecosystem — who was discussing Tesla, what they were claiming, whether anything was reaching the public that shouldn't. The "no action" and "closed" notations on individual reports are misleading; the master file remained open and under active surveillance through at least 1957.

## STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS: WHAT THE THREE PARTS ESTABLISH IN COMBINATION

Across all three parts, the documentary record now supports the following operational sequence:

**January 7-8, 1943:** Tesla dies. Within hours: Spanel (who had a financial relationship with Tesla's protege Fitzgerald since December 1942) contacts FBI, War Department, and VP Wallace's office. Wallace advisor Lozado tells Spanel government is "vitally interested." War Department Colonel Erskine calls FBI January 9. APC seizes effects January 9.

**January 26-27, 1943:** ONI (Willis George), APC, and Trump (OSRD/MIT) conduct the technical examination. Navy personnel in uniform with microfilm equipment. Floor supervisor shown "Federal Authorities." Trump produces dismissive evaluation covering only Manhattan Warehouse materials, explicitly excluding hotel basement trunks.

**1935 — already documented in Tesla's own papers:** Signed Amtorg contract ($25,000, teleforce specifications) found among the papers being evaluated. Trump abstracts it but dismisses it as probably "unworkable."

**April 1946 (or May 1945 per Rath):** Harvey Rath resigns from FBI. Tells Spanel the FBI was "stopped from a higher level." Tells Bergstresser to contact the NY office.

**October 1945:** Fitzgerald and Bergstresser visit FBI. Wright Field (Craigee letter) requests access to Tesla effects. FBI: no jurisdiction. Army confirms "death ray is only defense against atomic bomb."

**1950:** Kosanovich requests FBI microfilms. FBI denies. Manhattan Warehouse confirms Navy uniforms during the only examination. FBI's 1950 investigation closed.

**1951:** Rath re-interviewed, denies what Bergstresser said he said. Nichols briefs Tolson on Tesla case in preparation for Pegler meeting — confirms Navy microfilmed "all his papers" internally while denying it publicly.

**1957:** Tesla case file still active. Margaret Storm being monitored. File not closed.

**1983 (Part 1):** WPAFB researchers ask FBI about Tesla papers. Classified memo: Soviet directed energy programs influenced by Tesla work. Tesla papers reportedly went OAPC → WPAFB Equipment Lab (now closed). FBI still can't account for the papers 40 years after seizure.

## PATTERN: THE TECHNICAL DISMISSAL AS OPERATIONAL COVER

The Trump evaluation served a specific operational function that becomes clear in retrospect. By characterizing Tesla's recent work as "speculative, philosophical, and somewhat promotional," the evaluation:

1. Justified releasing most of the papers to APC rather than classifying them
2. Provided a public narrative for why the FBI had "no interest" in the effects
3. Created a documented professional opinion to cite against anyone pressing for investigation
4. Did so while simultaneously documenting (in the exhibit abstracts) the actual content of what was found — including the Amtorg contract, the British government offers, and the directed energy weapon specifications

The dismissal and the documentation coexist in the same document. Anyone reading only the conclusion gets "nothing of value." Anyone reading the exhibits gets the full picture of what Tesla had been doing.

This is not evidence that Trump was covering anything up. He may have been a genuinely skeptical technical evaluator. But the **function** of his evaluation regardless of his intent was to provide official cover for the seizure while the actual intelligence value (ONI microfilms, Amtorg contract documentation) was extracted through a parallel channel.

## FINAL SYNTHESIS: THE COMPLETE OPERATIONAL PICTURE

The three-part FBI vault file establishes the following with documentary confidence:

**What was seized:** All Tesla effects at Hotel New Yorker + Manhattan Warehouse (75+ items). Hotel basement trunks explicitly not examined.

**Who seized it:** APC (Gorsuch, King) with War Department backing.

**Who examined it:** ONI (Willis George + 2 Navy enlisted), APC (Newington, Hedetniemi), OSRD/MIT (Trump).

**What they found:** An Amtorg contract ($25,000, teleforce specifications), British government offers (1936-1938), directed energy weapon specifications, and extensive wireless power transmission materials. Trump abstracted all of these while dismissing their practical value.

**What they did with it:** Navy microfilmed "all his papers" (Nichols memo). Some materials were "retained as exhibits" by APC. The hotel basement trunks were never examined. The Governor Clinton Hotel safe deposit box (working model, $10,000+ to build) was not examined. In 1983, the papers were reportedly unlocatable at WPAFB.

**What the FBI did:** Monitored the network around Tesla (Kosanovich, Fitzgerald, Spanel, ASC). Provided consistent public denial. Kept the file open through at least 1957.

**The accountability gap:** Neither the Navy microfilms, the hotel basement trunk contents, nor the original signed Amtorg contract have been publicly accounted for. The 1983 classified FBI memo (Part 1) indicates the US government itself could not locate the papers 40 years later. Where they went from WPAFB Equipment Lab remains the primary open lead.
