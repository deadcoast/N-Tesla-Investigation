# Five Coil Investigation

> The documents confirm a response. Responses don't emerge from nothing. What they expose is the shape of what was being protected — and that shape points backward through history to things the vault never mentions.

## THE FIVE COILS

- COIL I — Financial Strangulation. JP Morgan funds Wardenclyffe in 1900, withdraws in 1902. The standard telling: Morgan lost interest when he realized wireless power couldn't be metered. The untold version: that is the motive. Metered electricity is the most successful recurring revenue model in industrial history. Wireless power doesn't just threaten it — it ends it permanently. Morgan didn't walk away from a failed project. He walked away from a project that worked exactly as designed and was therefore the most dangerous technology he'd ever funded. The $12M Westinghouse royalty surrender is framed as noble sacrifice. It should be read as a mechanism — Tesla kept at survival level, too poor to build, not poor enough to die, alive as a cautionary symbol.
- COIL II — Reputational Architecture. "Very eccentric if not mentally deranged" entered the federal record the night he died, sourced from hotel staff, before any investigation. That framing did not emerge organically. Someone needed it there, immediately, to pre-empt the question everyone was about to ask: why is the government seizing a dead man's papers at midnight? The answer pre-loaded into the file: because he was a confused old man, not because the work was dangerous. Meanwhile — and this is what history buried — the Supreme Court restored Tesla's radio patent in June 1943, six months after his death. The vindication he spent 39 years fighting for arrived while the Navy was microfilming his papers. He never knew. That timing is not coincidence. It's the closing of a parenthesis.
- COIL III — Technical Dismissal. Trump's evaluation is the most elegant part of the architecture. He covered 1928-1943 — exactly the Amtorg/teleforce period — with one sentence: "speculative, philosophical, promotional." That sentence is in the same document that abstracts a signed $25,000 contract with Soviet intelligence infrastructure for directed energy weapon specifications. The dismissal and the evidence coexist on the same pages. For anyone reading only the conclusion: nothing there. For anyone reading the exhibits: everything there. The evaluation is a magician's trick — it documents what it claims doesn't exist.
- COIL IV — Network Capture. Everyone in Tesla's orbit was under surveillance. Kosanovich, Fitzgerald, Spanel, Adamic, the ASC, Margaret Storm 14 years after death. This wasn't about those individuals. It was about ensuring that the network of people who could testify to what Tesla actually built — the working model, the teleforce papers, the Amtorg transaction — remained either compromised, discredited, or silent. Adamic dies August 1951. Five months before the Belgrade shipment. The connective tissue of the network is removed at the exact moment when accounting for the papers would have been possible.
- COIL V — Posthumous Erasure. The papers went somewhere. OAPC → Manhattan Warehouse (microfilmed by Navy) → Belgrade (partially, 60 of 75+ items) → WPAFB Equipment Lab (per 1983 classified memo, now closed) → unknown. The 1983 FBI memo shows the US government itself cannot locate them 40 years after seizure. This isn't bureaucratic incompetence. Bureaucracies don't lose things they don't want lost.

### WHAT HISTORY HAS MISSED — the six blind spots

1. The Marconi patent timing. June 1943. The Supreme Court ruled in Tesla's favor. The APC seized his papers in January 1943. The seizure came before the legal vindication. If the patent decision was anticipated — and it was, the case had been working through courts for years — then the seizure window was not accidental. The papers were secured before his vindication could generate public pressure to treat them as the work of a vindicated genius rather than a declining eccentric.
2. The simultaneous rejection pattern. Tesla offered teleforce to the US government (declined). To Britain (four letters, one polite rejection, 1936-1938). To the Soviets (paid $25,000 and then nothing). To Yugoslavia (subsidized at survival level). Every government with both the motive to want the technology and the resources to develop it declined simultaneously across a ten-year window. Two of those governments were active adversaries of each other. The probability that this represents independent technical judgment rather than coordinated action is close to zero.
3. The utility industry's fingerprints are absent from the record. The FBI vault is dense with surveillance of Tesla's Yugoslav-American network. It contains almost nothing about who in American industry had motive to ensure his wireless power work never reached completion. The Edison-Westinghouse-Morgan nexus that shaped his career is never examined in the files — only the foreign threat to his papers. The domestic financial suppression infrastructure is the invisible coil.
4. The working model. A device Tesla said cost over $10,000 to build, stored in the Governor Clinton Hotel safe deposit box since 1932 as collateral for a $400 debt. Trump's evaluation mentions it once and dismisses it as standard electrical measuring equipment. No subsequent document mentions it. It vanishes from the record. A physical object does not simply disappear. Someone took it, paid the $400 debt to release it, or it was removed without payment. There is no accounting.
5. The hotel basement trunks. Ten years untouched, explicitly excluded from Trump's evaluation. If the Manhattan Warehouse materials were "speculative and philosophical," what was in the trunks Tesla had been accumulating since before 1934 that he kept entirely separate? The most dangerous materials are typically the ones most carefully sequestered. Their fate is completely undocumented.
6. The Tesla-Adamic-Kosanovich triangle. These three men — the inventor, his Yugoslav-American literary champion, his politically ambitious nephew — form a triangle through which control of Tesla's posthumous legacy ran. All three are under FBI surveillance simultaneously. One dies (Tesla, January 1943). One dies under suspicious circumstances (Adamic, August 1951, ruled suicide). One survives, returns to Yugoslavia, falls "in disfavor" per FBI files, and apparently takes whatever he knew with him. The triangle closes without any corner ever speaking freely.

### WHERE "COILED LIES" GOES NEXT

The FBI vault is the government's layer. The next layer is the financial layer, and it predates the FBI by forty years.
Morgan Papers at the Pierpont Morgan Library. The Westinghouse corporate archive in Pittsburgh. The utility industry lobbying records from 1910-1940. The Marconi patent litigation funding sources. The British Foreign Office files on the 1936-1938 teleforce negotiations (UK National Archives, Kew — these have never been examined in any Tesla scholarship).
The thesis that emerges from all of this, applied to the book's framing: Tesla was not suppressed by one actor. He was suppressed by a structure. The structure had five components — financial, reputational, technical, network, and erasure — and each component was maintained by different actors with different motives who never needed to coordinate explicitly. The financial strangulation was Morgan and the utility industry. The reputational architecture was built by the government after his death. The technical dismissal was a single evaluation by a radar engineer in three days. The network capture was FBI counterintelligence standard operating procedure applied to anyone Yugoslav-adjacent during the Cold War. The erasure was institutional inertia and classification.
None of these actors needed to know what the others were doing. The coil wound itself.
That's the tragedy. And that's the title.
