## CONFIRMED FINDINGS — HIGH CONFIDENCE

### 1. NAVAL MICROFILM — EXPLICITLY CONFIRMED IN INTERNAL FBI MEMO

**Page 28 — Nichols to Tolson, January 30, 1951:**

> "It was very clear we had no responsibility for Tesla's effects, that the Alien Property Custodian seized them and **we learned later that Naval authorities made microfilms of all his papers.**"

This is an internal FBI memo written by Assistant Director L.B. Nichols to Associate Director Clyde Tolson. It is not a public statement. It is not the template denial. It is a frank operational summary for Hoover's closest deputy.

"All his papers" — not some. Not selected papers. **All.**

This directly contradicts the public narrative ("we weren't involved") while being internally accurate (the FBI wasn't involved — the Navy was). The Navy extraction is now confirmed in the FBI's own private communication.

Cross-reference: Part 1 confirmed Willis George (ONI) was physically present at the January 26-27 examination with photographic/microfilm equipment. This memo confirms the outcome of that operation.

### 2. HARVEY F. RATH — THE FBI AGENT WHO WENT SILENT

**Page 28-29 — Nichols to Tolson, January 30, 1951:**

> "We did have an Agent, Harvey F. Rath, who entered on duty February 16, 1942, resigned April 5, 1946. At the time [of his departure] his business [address] was [redacted] Avenue... his residence was [redacted] Street, Orange, New Jersey."

This is notable because Spanel's communications (furnished to the Department, September 19, 1950) stated that **Harvey Rath was the FBI agent he dealt with on the Tesla case**, and that Rath had:

- Resigned
- Told Spanel he could not discuss the matter
- **"Their hands were tied, that there was nothing the FBI could do as they had been stopped from a higher level"**
- "Requested never to discuss the matter with him again"

Nichols notes: **"The file does not indicate that we have ever interviewed Rath on the basis of..."** — the sentence is cut, but the implication is clear: Rath left and was never debriefed on what he told Spanel.

**Assessment:** Harvey Rath is a critical figure. He was the operational FBI agent on the Tesla case. He resigned April 1946 — three years after Tesla's death, one year after the war ended. He explicitly told a civilian informant that the FBI was "stopped from a higher level." Nichols acknowledges this claim exists in the files but deflects. The FBI never interviewed Rath to establish what he told Spanel or why.

**NEW LEAD (LED-015):** Harvey F. Rath. FBI agent February 1942 – April 1946. Residence: Orange, New Jersey. Told Spanel FBI was stopped "from a higher level" on Tesla case. Never re-interviewed. His personnel file and any exit interview should exist in FBI/DOJ records.

### 3. "STOPPED FROM A HIGHER LEVEL" — SPANEL'S SENATE COMMUNICATIONS

**Page 28 — same Nichols memo:**

> "He further stated that he was associated with Fitzgerald and as such came in touch with Tesla. **He stated the FBI investigated this matter but their hands were tied, that there was nothing the FBI could do as they had been stopped from a higher level**, that Harvey R[ath] the FBI agent he dealt with, resigned and requested never to discuss the matter with him again, that [Rath had a family] to consider and that **the last hope was Congress**."

Spanel communicated this to Senators (unnamed in file). These communications were "furnished to the Department on September 19, 1950."

**Assessment:** "Stopped from a higher level" is not an allegation from a conspiracy theorist — it is a claim made by Abraham Spanel, who was personally involved in the Tesla case from January 1943, who met with the FBI, who was in contact with Wallace's office, and who later communicated this to both FBI agents and US Senators. The FBI's own internal file records this claim without rebuttal.

The question is: higher than what level? Higher than the FBI field office = Bureau HQ or above. Given Hoover's personal involvement in Tesla-related correspondence, "higher" likely means either an inter-agency authority (OSRD, ONI, OWI) or White House-level direction.

### 4. COLONEL ERSKINE — WAR DEPARTMENT NOTIFICATION CONFIRMED

**Page 27 — Nichols to Tolson:**

> "Our files also reflect that Colonel Erskine of HQ Military Intelligence called on January 9, 1943, advising that Tesla had died, that Spanel had communicated with the War Department regarding this death, that Tesla had a nephew named Sava Kosanovich who had taken possession of Tesla's papers and Spanel thought the papers might be used against our Government."

War Department Military Intelligence was notified by **January 9, 1943** — the day of the APC seizure. Erskine's notification confirms the chain: Spanel → War Department → FBI, all on the same day. This is concurrent with the Lozado (Wallace advisor) notification confirmed in Part 1.

The War Department, VP Wallace's office, and the FBI were all in motion within 24 hours of Tesla's death.

### 7. COLONEL GROMBACH MEMO — TESLA MISLOCATED TO CLEVELAND

**Page 30 — War Department intelligence memo, August 30, 1946:**

An internal War Department memo (Grombach, Special Intelligence) passed through the State Department mentions an individual described as:

> "collaborating [with]... **Nicolas Tesla the well known scientist residing in Cleveland, Ohio**, probably American citizen"

Two problems: Tesla died in January 1943. Tesla lived in New York, not Cleveland.

**Assessment:** This 1946 War Department intelligence memo describes Tesla as if he is still alive and living in Cleveland. This is either:

1. A clerical/intelligence error — conflating Tesla with someone else
2. A reference to Tesla's papers or legacy work, poorly worded
3. A deliberate misdirection in the intelligence record

Most likely option 1 or 2 — but the existence of a 1946 War Department intelligence memo discussing "Tesla" in active terms, 3 years after his death, is notable. Who was the unnamed individual "collaborating" with Tesla's work in Cleveland? Cleveland is where Kosanovich surveillance was also active (the 1943 Slovak rally, ADAMIC connections). This may be a reference to someone working with Tesla's ideas or unpublished papers rather than Tesla himself.

### 8. LOUIS ADAMIC — THE CONNECTING NODE

Multiple surveillance subjects in Part 2 are connected through **Louis Adamic**, the Slovenian-American author:

- Adamic is explicitly named in the Kosanovich surveillance file as the organizer of Yugoslav relief rallies
- The unnamed loyalty investigation subject (pages 32-41) met Kosanovich through Adamic's network
- Adamic published "The Slavic American" — listed in the ASC surveillance material
- Adamic organized the 1943 Cleveland rally for Yugoslav relief

**Louis Adamic died August 4, 1951** — officially ruled a suicide, a gunshot wound at his New Jersey farm. His death came 5 months after Kosanovich's ambassadorship ended and 5 months before Tesla's effects were shipped to Belgrade. The timing of Adamic's death within this network's operational period warrants notation.

### 9. FITZGERALD — "DEATH RAY IS ONLY DEFENSE AGAINST ATOM BOMBS"

**Page 28 — Nichols memo:**

> "In 1945, we talked to a Private Bloyce Fitzgerald, who stated he had been associated with Tesla, and that **the Army believed that Tesla's 'death ray' is the only defense against atom bombs.**"

This is now the third independent confirmation of this claim (Part 1 also contained it, and the 1945 FBI letter). "The Army believed" — not just Fitzgerald personally. This is Army institutional assessment circa 1945.

The timing: August 1945, atom bombs drop. By October 1945, Fitzgerald shows up at the FBI with Craigee's letter requesting Tesla's papers. The Army's interest in Tesla's directed energy work intensified precisely when the atomic bomb made conventional air defense obsolete.
