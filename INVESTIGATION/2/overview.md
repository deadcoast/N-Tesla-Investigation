# Part 2  Overview

---

**The single most important sentence in Part 2 is on page 28** — Nichols writing privately to Tolson:

*"It was very clear we had no responsibility for Tesla's effects, that the Alien Property Custodian seized them and we learned later that Naval authorities made microfilms of **all** his papers."*

That's the Assistant Director of the FBI telling the Associate Director, in a private memo that was never meant for public consumption, that the Navy microfilmed everything. The word "all" is doing enormous work there. Compare it to 40 years of public letters saying "we weren't involved."

---

**Harvey F. Rath is now the most urgent lead in the entire investigation.** He's the FBI agent who actually worked the Tesla case. He resigned April 1946. He personally told Spanel the FBI was "stopped from a higher level." He told Spanel never to contact him again and cited his family. The FBI's own file notes that he was never re-interviewed after he said this. That's not an oversight — agents who make unauthorized disclosures get interviewed. The file is silent on him after his resignation date.

LED-015 is now highest priority alongside LED-011 (WPAFB Equipment Lab).

---

**The Silvermaster meeting** (LED-016) is the sharpest intelligence angle in the file. Kosanovich met a confirmed Soviet spy in December 1946 while Tesla's papers were still in US storage. The FBI documented it and apparently didn't follow it. Whether that's because they couldn't make a case or because someone didn't want a case made is the question.
