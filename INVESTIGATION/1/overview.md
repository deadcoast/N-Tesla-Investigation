# Part 1 Overview

---

**CONFIRMED — things we suspected now have primary source evidence:**

The Trump evaluation explicitly excluded the hotel basement trunks in his own words: *"no investigation was made of material in trunks which had remained untouched in the basement of the New Yorker Hotel for ten years."* PAT-001 isn't inference anymore — it's documented.

LED-004 is resolved. Kenneth M. Swezey. Brooklyn. Writer for Popular Mechanics. He was physically present when the safe was opened, took the birthday greetings book, and six months later wrote directly to Hoover asking what happened to the Edison Medal. Hoover personally signed the form-letter denial.

The Craigee/Wright Field event confirmed. October 1945. Fitzgerald shows up at the FBI's New York office with a military contingent and a letter signed by Brigadier General Craigee, Chief Engineering Division Wright Field. The FBI says not our problem, refer to APC. The file doesn't record whether they ever got access.

---

**NEW — things the file added that weren't in prior research:**

The 1983 classified Cincinnati FBI memo is the biggest surprise in Part 1. A WPAFB physicist from the Foreign Technology Division named Butler — working on Soviet directed energy weapons — tells the FBI that Tesla papers were reportedly sent from the OAPC to the "Equipment Lab" at WPAFB, "now closed or reorganized." The US government is still looking for these papers internally **forty years after seizure**. LED-001 is upgraded to highest priority.

The January 26-27 examination team included Naval Intelligence (Willis George, ONI Third Naval District) and two Navy chief yeomen. The warehouse supervisor saw the men in Navy uniforms and microfilm equipment. The FBI explicitly says it didn't participate — which is technically accurate. ONI did. That's LED-010, and it's now a critical authorization chain question.

The Edison Medal is a new physical lead (LED-014). Swezey confirms it was in the safe. Keys and medal both missing when the safe arrived in Belgrade in 1951. Keys turned up in a tin box outside the safe. Medal was never found. Something opened that safe during the transit window.
