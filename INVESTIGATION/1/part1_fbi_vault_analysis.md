## STRUCTURAL OVERVIEW

Part 1 spans 1940–1983. It includes:

- Incoming civilian tips about the teleforce (1940)
- Operational teletypes from the night of death through Jan 12, 1943
- The Trump evaluation letter and exhibits (Jan 30, 1943)
- The Craigee/Wright Field access request (Oct 1945)
- Decades of public inquiry letters + FBI's templated denial response
- A 1983 classified document from Cincinnati regarding WPAFB researchers

The file is both an operational record and a narrative management record. The FBI's public response to all external inquiries is **identical and templated** across 40 years: "effects were impounded by the Office of Alien Property, not the FBI."

## CONFIRMED FINDINGS — HIGH CONFIDENCE

### 1. DEATH DATE DISCREPANCY — **DOCUMENT-CONFIRMED**

**Page 5 teletype (Jan 12, 1943):**
> "INQUIRY DEVELOPS THAT TESLA DIED JAN EIGHT, RATHER THAN THURSDAY, JAN SEVEN, AS STATED IN REFERENCE TELETYPE."

The **original** FBI report said Jan 7. The correction teletype says Jan 8.  
**Page 9 internal memo (Jan 11, 1943) still says Jan 7.**  
The O'Neill biography (1944) and a 1967 Oregon State inquiry letter also say **January 8.**

**Assessment:** The government's own internal document stream contains a death-date discrepancy that was never resolved. The correction memo is explicit. The subsequent memos ignore it. This is either administrative sloppiness or deliberate ambiguity. Given VP Wallace was personally notified, the exact moment of death had operational significance. EVT-001 needs a flag: **DATE-AMBIGUITY-INTERNAL**.

### 3. TRUMP EVALUATION — **SCOPE LIMITATION CONFIRMED IN HIS OWN WORDS**

**Page 178-179 — Trump's own letter, January 30, 1943:**

> "For reasons indicated below, no investigation was made of material **in trunks which had remained untouched in the basement of the New Yorker Hotel for ten years** prior to Dr. Tesla's death."

This is definitive. Trump **explicitly excluded** the hotel basement trunks. He only examined what the APC had already seized and moved to Manhattan Warehouse. The hotel basement trunks — stored there since approximately **1934** — were not part of his evaluation.

**Trump's title in this letter:** "Technical Aide, Division 14, NDRC"  
Division 14 of NDRC = Radar and Detection. Not the electrical engineering division one would expect for this evaluation.

**Trump's language about the excluded period:**
> "his thoughts and efforts during **at least the past fifteen years** were primarily of a speculative, philosophical, and somewhat promotional character"

Fifteen years back from 1943 = **1928**. Trump dismisses 1928–1943. That is precisely the period where Tesla's most advanced work on teleforce, wireless transmission, and unconventional physics would reside. Trump is not claiming there is nothing — he is claiming what he examined contains nothing new. He did not examine the 1928–1943 hotel trunks.

**PAT-001 fully operational:** The evaluation provided deniability while the hotel basement trunks remained unreviewed and in a completely different custody chain.

### 6. CRAIGEE AND WRIGHT FIELD — **CONFIRMED AND EXPANDED**

**Page 127 — October 26, 1945:**

> "On October 26, 1945, Eloyce Fitzgerald, U.S. Army, a young scientist who had been Tesla's protege, called in person at our New York Field office. With him were [names redacted], a title from a research development unit at **Wright Field, Dayton, Ohio**. These men carried a letter signed by **Brigadier-General In-Cr-Craijea, Chief Engineering Division, Wright Field**, requesting that the FBI allow the bearers of the letter access to the effects of Nikola Tesla."

FBI response: "FBI had no jurisdiction over Tesla's effects" — referred to APC.  
**"Bureau files do not indicate whether the men ever examined Tesla's belongings."**

This is the Craigee assessment. He signed the letter but the file does not contain his evaluation. Our database has his verbal assessment ("particle beam weapon is real") from secondary sourcing — but the actual Craigee report, if it exists, is not in Part 1. It may be in Part 2 or Part 3, or in NARA RG342 (Air Force records).

**Fitzgerald context (pages 174-177):** By October 1945, Fitzgerald was at Wright Field "engaged in a highly secret experimental project." He was "working on the perfection of Tesla's death ray which in Fitzgerald's opinion is the only possible defense against offensive use by another nation of the Atomic Bomb." He had interested "a group of young Army scientists" at Wright Field in a foundation to secure Tesla's effects.

The man who personally told Tesla about his completed wireless transmission experiments is now at Wright Field after the bomb drops, trying to access Tesla's papers, carrying a letter from the Chief Engineering Division general. This is not a curiosity visit.

### 7. WRIGHT PATTERSON — **1983 CLASSIFIED DOCUMENT CONFIRMS PAPER TRANSFER**

**Pages 133-135 — Classified SECRET, August 1983, Cincinnati FBI:**

Two WPAFB researchers contacted FBI:

- A physicist from the **Electro Optics Research Lab, Avionics Division, USAF** at WPAFB
- **Butler**, physicist and intelligence specialist, **Foreign Technology Division** at WPAFB

Butler's assessment (reported in the classified FBI memo):
> "The Soviet Union has allegedly had access to some of Tesla's papers, possibly in Belgrade and/or elsewhere, which influenced their **early research into directed energy weapons**."

Butler was "unable to locate any Tesla papers or copies of same in the classified or unclassified libraries at WPAFB." However:

> "There are reports that some portions of them were shipped by the Custodian of Alien Property Office in Washington, D.C. to **a technical research lab at WPAFB, possibly the 'Equipment Lab', now closed for some years or reorganized into another organization.**"

**This is an official 1983 classified FBI document confirming:**

1. Tesla papers were reportedly sent from OAPC to WPAFB
2. The receiving lab has been closed or reorganized (i.e., the trail ends administratively)
3. Soviet directed energy weapons programs may have benefited from Belgrade access
4. The US government's own intelligence community was still searching for these papers **40 years after seizure**

LED-001 (Wright Patterson) is **upgraded to HIGHEST priority** — this is no longer secondary-sourced.

### 8. SPANEL-WALLACE-LOZADO CHAIN — **CONFIRMED**

**Page 7 teletype:**
> "Spanel was also in telephonic communication with Dr. Lozado, one of the advisors to Vice President Wallace concerning this matter, and Lozado told Spanel that the government was vitally interested in the effects of Tesla and requested Spanel to lose no time in doing all he could to preserve them."

VP Wallace's office was directly notified and actively interested on the **day of death** (or the day after). Lozado is an intermediary — the question is whether Wallace's office learned from Lozado or tasked Lozado. Given that Wallace was personally notified (as we established in prior research), Lozado was likely reporting up rather than initiating.

### 9. FBI INTERNAL DESCRIPTION — CONTRADICTS PUBLIC POSITION

**Page 72 internal file note (repeated across multiple years):**
> "Bufile 100-2237 reflects that Dr. Tesla was a world-famous electrical inventor and at the time of his death, **all of his personal papers and effects were believed dangerous to the country's Security if they fell into unauthorized hands.**"

This is the internal FBI description of the situation. The public response to every inquiry was "we weren't involved." The internal description acknowledges that the papers were **believed to be a national security matter.**

The two positions are not technically incompatible — the APC handled it while the FBI monitored. But the FBI's consistent public denial is calibrated to discourage further inquiry. Each inquiry gets the identical letter. Hoover signed many of them personally.

### 10. AMTORG — TESLA-SOVIET CONTACT DOCUMENTED

**Trump Exhibit D** — catalogued among Tesla's papers:
> "Reply to Amtorg re the generation of high-voltage and the acceleration of charged particles — This document, dated **November 8, 1935**, answers questions raised by Soviet engineers and scientists regarding Tesla's proposal of May 16, 1935."

Amtorg was the Soviet trade organization in New York, used extensively for intelligence. Tesla was in written dialogue with Soviet engineers about charged particle acceleration in 1935. This is why the 1983 Butler memo can plausibly claim Soviet directed energy programs were influenced by Tesla's work. The conduit was two-directional: Tesla sent proposals to the Soviets; the Soviets sent questions back. Trump catalogued this and apparently considered it of no technical value. Butler, 48 years later, does not agree.

## UPDATED REDACTION ANALYSIS

### RED-001 UPDATE (Torpedo device)

The torpedo device is now more specifically identified. Fitzgerald told FBI that Tesla had "conceived and perfected a revolutionary type of torpedo which is not presently in use by any nation." The word "nations" is where the redaction cuts in the original Hoover-signed memo. After "not in use by any —" the sentence breaks. Now we know what was cut: the word "nations" and presumably the device's characteristics. Trump catalogued **none** of the torpedo specifications in his exhibits. The torpedo documents are not in what Trump reviewed — or they were reviewed and not catalogued.

### RED-002 UPDATE (Kosanovich theft allegation)

Page 10 reveals what was planned: "The New York Office was instructed to **discreetly** move the matter with the State's Attorney in New York City with the view to possibly taking Kosanovich into custody on a **burglary charge** and obtaining the various papers which Kosanovich is reported to have taken." This never happened. The resolution of why it didn't happen = RED-002. The file does not show any follow-up. Kosanovich became Yugoslav Ambassador to the US and eventually sent the effects to Belgrade.

## BEHAVIORAL OBSERVATIONS

**Pattern of Hoover personal signature:**  
Hoover personally signed letters to Swezey (1955), to multiple public inquiries (1952, 1964, 1967, 1969). This is above the normal delegation level for inquiry responses. Each letter is accompanied by an internal note that uses language very close to "papers were believed dangerous to national security." Hoover is personally managing the public narrative while the internal files acknowledge the security stakes.

**The FBI as narrative manager, not investigator:**  
Part 1 reveals that the FBI's primary role in the Tesla case after January 1943 was **responding to inquiries with a consistent cover story.** The operational action (seizing effects, evaluating contents, microfilming, transferring to WPAFB) was done by APC + Navy + OSRD. The FBI's job was to maintain the story that it had nothing to do with any of it. The identical templated response across 40 years of inquiries is not bureaucratic inertia — it is consistent narrative discipline.

**The 1983 classified memo breaks the pattern:**  
When actual government researchers (WPAFB, classified programs) start asking in 1983, the FBI doesn't use the template. The Cincinnati office files a classified report and asks for guidance. The Bureau is now caught between its 40-year public narrative and the operational reality that its own Air Force colleagues can't find the papers and believe they went to WPAFB via OAPC. The irony is that Butler's classified request for access to FBI files in 1983 may have produced exactly what journalists and academics couldn't get for 40 years.
