## ENT-001 — NIKOLA TESLA

The central question about Tesla's decline is not financial incompetence. Men who produce 300+ patents across 40 years do not simply fail to monetize their work by accident. The pattern of his poverty maps almost perfectly onto the withdrawal of Morgan funding in 1904. Before Morgan: celebrated, funded, building. After Morgan: isolated, increasingly dismissed, dying broke.

The press reframing from genius to eccentric is not gradual. It tracks the funding withdrawal with a lag of roughly 3-5 years — the time it takes for a reputational shift to normalize in the absence of counter-narrative. Nobody coordinated this. The institutions that would have amplified a positive Tesla narrative (Morgan-aligned press, utility-adjacent publications) simply stopped. The vacuum filled with eccentricity stories because those were more interesting than "successful inventor makes incremental progress."

The death date discrepancy deserves more attention than it gets. Jan 7 official, Jan 8 in FBI documents. In a normal death this is clerical. In a death where papers were seized within hours and the FBI was personally notified at VP level, a date discrepancy is operational. Did something happen between Jan 7 and Jan 8 that the official record is smoothing over?

**Critical gap:** What was Tesla working on in 1942-43? His final year is the least documented period of his most relevant work. His public claims from 1934 onward were specific. His private notebooks from that final period are what's missing. That is not coincidence.

## ENT-002 — J. EDGAR HOOVER

Hoover's personal signature on a field memo is the single most anomalous documented fact in the released files. Understand what this means procedurally: the Director of the FBI does not sign memos about individual field cases. His signature on the Jan 11 1943 Tesla torpedo memo means one of two things. Either someone above him — Wallace, FDR level — required his personal certification, which means the Tesla material was reaching the top of wartime government in real time. Or Hoover had personal reasons to be involved that had nothing to do with the official chain of command.

Hoover's personal files were destroyed by Helen Gandy in 1972 after his death. Those files contained decades of illegal surveillance on thousands of Americans. Tesla had an active FBI surveillance file since at least 1940. Whether a parallel personal Hoover file existed on Tesla is unknown. That it might have is consistent with Hoover's documented behavior on dozens of other subjects.

The Kenneth [REDACTED] cross-reference is the sharpest available lead from Hoover's behavior. He personally signed a memo linking Tesla to this individual. The name is blacked out in released files. In 1943, if a name remains classified 80 years later, it either connects to still-operational intelligence or it connects to something the government has never officially acknowledged. Kenneth Swezey is the primary candidate. His own FBI file, if it exists, is directly FOIAble.

## ENT-003 — SAVA KOSANOVIĆ

The theft allegation narrative has a fundamental logical problem: Kosanović was never prosecuted. He remained a US-recognized diplomat, served as Yugoslav Ambassador 1946-1948, and successfully repatriated Tesla's estate to Belgrade. Men who steal classified wartime documents do not then get to serve as foreign ambassadors in the country they allegedly stole from.

Either the theft was minor, opportunistic, and the FBI used it to justify custody, or the theft was real and the government chose not to prosecute for reasons that would have been more embarrassing than the theft itself. The heavily redacted resolution document (RED-002) hides which of these is true.

His death in 1956, one year after the museum opened, has not been independently verified in any source reviewed so far. This is not an accusation. It is a documented gap. Men who die one year after completing the project that defined their life do not automatically die of suspicious causes. But verifying the cause of death is basic due diligence.

The Belgrade angle is underexplored by Western researchers. Kosanović's diplomatic cables, if accessible through Serbian archives, would document what he believed the US government extracted. His personal correspondence to the Tesla Museum collection almost certainly addresses the trunk discrepancy. Nobody has publicly reported examining it.

## ENT-004 — DR. JOHN G. TRUMP

The conflict of interest is documented and specific. Tesla publicly demonstrated that two small Tesla coils could achieve what Trump's massive Van de Graaff generator achieved — in front of witnesses, at MIT. This is a professional humiliation of the first order in a field where reputation is everything. Trump then becomes the sole evaluator of Tesla's lifetime work, produces a negative finding in two days, and that finding becomes the permanent official record.

The physical impossibility of the evaluation is worth stating clearly. At eight hours per day over two days, sixteen hours total, for eighty trunks — that is twelve minutes per trunk. Each trunk contained documents, notebooks, drawings, correspondence, and equipment components. Twelve minutes per trunk is not analysis. It is selection. Trump looked at what he was directed to look at, or what remained after prior extraction, and dismissed it.

His subsequent career in cancer radiation therapy used high-voltage electrical equipment — Tesla's precise domain. Whether the evaluation informed his subsequent research is not provable without his personal papers. But the career trajectory deserves noting.

The authorization document — who specifically directed Trump to perform this evaluation, what his written instructions were, what he was told he was looking for — is not in the released files. Finding that document is LED-002 and it is the most surgically precise lead in the entire investigation.

## ENT-007 — VANNEVAR BUSH

This is the most underinvestigated node. Bush simultaneously controlled:
- NDRC, which Trump reported to and which authorized the Tesla evaluation
- The Manhattan Project apparatus, which was the most classified weapons research program in US history, directly adjacent to Tesla's particle beam and directed energy work

The overlap is too structurally significant to be unexamined. If Bush authorized Trump's selection (LED-002), and Bush also controlled classified weapons programs that could absorb Tesla's work, then the "nothing of value" evaluation was not an assessment — it was a transfer protocol. The official record closes the file. The classified program opens a new one with the same material under different authority.

Bush's personal papers at MIT are the most accessible primary source for this hypothesis. He was a meticulous correspondent. Scientists of his generation documented their reasoning in letters. If he privately assessed Tesla's work as valuable, the trace is there.

## ENT-008 — BRIG. GEN. CRAIGEE

The most important thing about Craigee's assessment is not the assessment itself but its fate. A military General, the first officer to fly a jet aircraft, with weapons evaluation expertise — concluded the particle beam weapon was real. This assessment was given zero official weight against the conclusion of a civilian electrical engineer with a documented grudge.

That inversion — military weapons expert overridden by civilian grudge-holder — does not happen by accident in wartime bureaucracy. Wartime weapons assessments go to military evaluators by default. Trump's involvement was the anomaly, not Craigee's. Craigee's assessment was the normal channel. Trump's was the inserted one.

The primary document of Craigee's assessment has not been located. Marc Seifer references it in his biography. Finding the source document Seifer used would either confirm or refine this entire line of analysis.
